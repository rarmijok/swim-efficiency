8-Week Plan · Pool + Dryland

Raise the turnover,
hold the length.

Your stroke length is already strong — about 1.79 m per stroke. Your speed is capped by turnover: you cruise near 19.6 strokes/min, and your rate sags in the back of long swims. This block lifts your stroke rate toward 22–24/min while protecting the length you've built, pairing three pool sessions a week with two short dryland sessions that raise your power ceiling and keep your shoulders durable. Everything is anchored to one test you run in Week 1.

02

Two tests: Week 1 and Week 8

Critical Swim Speed (your pace anchor)

Warm up well. Swim a 400m time trial all-out; rest 5+ min; swim a 200m all-out.

CSS pace /100m = (400m time − 200m time) ÷ 2

Every "@ CSS" below means this pace. Re-test in Week 8 — it should drop. Your best-effort history suggests a starting CSS near 2:21–2:25; trust the test over the estimate.

Stroke-count baseline (your length guardrail)

On an easy 100m, count strokes per length (you're around 14 for 25m). Note it.

The golden rule of this whole block: when you add tempo, your strokes-per-length must not climb more than a stroke or two. Faster rate at the same count = faster swimming. Faster rate with a ballooning count = thrashing. Watch this number every tempo set.

Guardrails

Make it stick, stay healthy

  • Speed over load — light resistance moved fast builds turnover; heavy & slow doesn't.
  • Prehab is mandatory — adding fast pulling volume is what triggers swimmer's shoulder. Sharp shoulder pain = stop.
  • Don't stack the explosive dryland day before the speed swim; keep one easy day before the threshold swim.
  • One full rest day a week — the gains land during recovery, not the session.
  • Land builds the engine; the pool spends it — carry every cord turnover session into the water that week.
Not medical advice — adjust to how your body responds, and see a coach for live stroke feedback when you can. Re-test Week 8 and reload the tracker to watch rate & length climb together · page 3 of 3
